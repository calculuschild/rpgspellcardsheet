CASTING A SPELL
If your spell says to do a melee or ranged spell attack, follow the same steps as for a normal attack. Otherwise, your spell automatically hits.
CONCENTRATION
Some spells require concentration to keep them active; losing concentration ends the spell. Such spells are marked with the (C) bubble. You can end concentration at any time (no action required). If you cast another spell that requires concentration, the first spell ends. You also lose concentration if you are killed or incapacitated. If you take damage, you must make a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage, whichever is higher.
RITUAL CASTING
Some spells can be cast as a ritual if desired. Doing so adds 10 minutes to the casting time, but does not expend a spell slot. These are marked with the (R) bubble.
COMPONENTS
A spell’s components are the physical requirements you must meet in order to cast it. Verbal (V) requires you to be able to speak, Somatic (S) requires a free hand to gesture, and Material (M) requires a component pouch or spellcasting focus, or sometimes specific items which are consumed.
SPELL SLOTS
Spells can be cast a limited number of times, represented as "slots." In order to cast a spell, you must have an open slot of the appropriate level or higher, which is then filled. Low-level spells cast using higher slots often makes the spell more powerful.
